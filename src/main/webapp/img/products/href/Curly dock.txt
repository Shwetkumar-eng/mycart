Rumex crispus (	Curly dock or yellow dock)

	The plant produces an inflorescence or flower stalk that grows to 1.5 metres (5 feet) high.
	It has smooth leaves shooting off from a large basal rosette, with distinctive waved or curled edges
	these can grow to 14–24 centimetres (5+1/2–9+1/2 inches).
	On the stalk, flowers and seeds are produced in clusters on branched stems, with the largest cluster being found at the apex.

Uses of Rumex ceispus :-

	Traditional folks in different parts of the world use Rumex crispus L. for the treatment of microbial infections, malaria, and sleeping sickness in the form of decoction or tincture.

Advanatage :-

	.Gastrointestinal tract disorders
	.Antihelminthic diseases
	.Arthritis
	.Inflammation
	.Bacterial infections
	.Sexually transmitted diseases

Disadvantage :-

	.It's toxic to cats
	.Ingesting any part of the plant can cause vomiting, cramps, diarrhea, and potentially fatal conditions
	.It's problematic on dairy farms and in organic agriculture
	.It's difficult to control effectively without using chemical herbicides
