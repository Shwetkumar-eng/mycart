LAURUS NOBILIS (Bay laurel)

       "The laurel is an evergreen shrub or small tree, variable in size and sometimes reaching 7–18 m (23–59 ft) tall.
	The genus Laurus includes three accepted species,[6] whose diagnostic key characters often overlap
	It is native to the Mediterranean region and is used as bay leaf for seasoning in cooking
	Laurus nobilis figures prominently in classical Greco-Roman culture
	The fruit is a small, shiny black drupe-like berry
	The bay laurel is dioecious (unisexual), with male and female flowers on separate plants".

USES OF BAY LAUREL :-
			Commonly used in cooking to add flavor and fragrance to soups, stews, braises, and pâtés. 
			Fresh bay leaves have more flavor.

			Bay leaves are an effective remedy to treat digestive problems such as flatulence and heartburn
			Folk remedy for rashes caused by poison ivy, poison oak, and stinging nettle 
			Repelling pests such as silverfish or stored product insects.

ADVANTAGE:-
		.Bay leaves are a boon for digestive health, stimulating enzyme production for better digestion.
		.Their anti-inflammatory compounds can ease conditions like arthritis. 
		.Managing diabetes, bay leaves have shown potential in regulating blood sugar levels and improving insulin sensitivity.

DISADVANTAGE :-

		.Very high amounts can cause sleepiness
		.It can also cause gastrointestinal symptoms, like diarrhea or bloating, as well as headaches.
		.Its influence on blood sugars, excessive consumption can lead to hypoglycemia.
		.Due to its ability to slow down the nervous system.
