Salix alba (White willow)

	Salix alba, the white willow, is a species of willow native to Europe and western and central Asia.
	The name derives from the white tone to the undersides of the leaves.

Uses of Salix alba :-

	The wood is tough, strong, and light in weight, but has minimal resistance to decay.
	The stems (withies) from coppiced and pollarded plants are used for basket-making.
	Charcoal made from the wood was important for gunpowder manufacture. 

Advantage :-
	
	.Wood: The wood is lightweight and durable, making it a popular material for baskets and cricket bats
	.Root growth: The bark contains indole-3-butyric acid, a plant hormone that stimulates root growth.
	.Edible uses: The inner bark, leaves, and shoots are edible.
	.Medicinal uses: Willow bark has been used for centuries to treat a variety of ailments, including back pain, osteoarthritis, fever, flu, and muscle pain.

Disadvantage :-

	.Underwatering: Leaves may wilt if the plant is underwatered.
	.Sap-sucking insects: These insects can create clusters of small yellow or white spots on the leaves.
	.Leaf rot: A pathogen can cause the leaves to rot.
