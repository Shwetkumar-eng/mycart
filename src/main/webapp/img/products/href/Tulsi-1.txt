OCIMUM TENUIFLORUM (Tulsi or holy basil)

	Ocimum tenuiflorum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is an aromatic perennial plant in the family Lamiaceae.
	It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia, Malesia, Asia, and the western Pacific.
	It is widely cultivated throughout the Southeast Asian tropics.

USES OF OCIMUM TENUIFLORUM :-

	Leaves: Can treat colds, coughs, indigestion, and chest ailments
	Flowers: Can treat bronchitis
	Seeds: Can treat malaria
	Extracts: Can treat stomach ulcers and eye diseases
	Entire plant: Can treat nausea and vomiting

ADVANTAGE :-

	.Insect repellent: Dried tulsi leaves can be mixed with stored grains to repel insects
	.Disinfection: Tulsi extracts can be used to disinfect water
	.Anti-inflammatory: Tulsi has anti-inflammatory properties that can help treat colds, coughs, and respiratory infections
	.Antioxidants: Tulsi is rich in antioxidants and can help the body detox
	.Antibacterial, antiviral, and antifungal: Tulsi has antibacterial, antiviral, and antifungal properties 

DISADVANTAGE :-

	.Increased uterine contractions, which can cause problems during labor or menstruation after pregnancy
	.Blood thinning, which is why it's avoided in anti-clotting drugs
	.Negative effects on male fertility if used in excess
