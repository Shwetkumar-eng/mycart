Salvia officinalis (Sage)

	Salvia officinalis, the common sage or sage, is a perennial, evergreen subshrub, with woody stems, grayish leaves, and blue to purplish flowers. 
	It is a member of the mint family Lamiaceae and native to the Mediterranean region, though it has been naturalized in many places throughout the world. 
	It has a long history of medicinal and culinary use, and in modern times it has been used as an ornamental garden plan.
